The Problem

HighFive Healthcare is a PE-backed Dental Services Organization scaling from 62 to 100+ offices on a $100M Norwest investment. Every new practice acquisition compounds manual overhead — 40-minute referral processes, hand-keyed data between CareStack, Salesforce, and Swell, zero cross-office enterprise reporting. The MuleSoft platform was purchased for a future integration vision that never materialized: 0% utilization on 5 of 7 core metrics, 2 support cases in 24 months.

Cost of the Current Path

Specialized Dental Partners — same founding year (2018), same PE model, now 3.6× larger at 275+ offices — hit the integration wall with 12 fragmented PMS systems and no unified data layer. Their CEO publicly acknowledged in January 2026: "We're going to make mistakes" on technology. Reactive band-aids (Pearly for A/R, Coupa for spend) cost more and solve less than a proactive integration layer. HighFive is at the exact inflection point where the foundation is cheaper to build at 62 offices than at 200.

Highest Risk

Agent sprawl without governance. CareStack VoiceStack™ (Agent #1), Salesforce Agentforce (Agent #2), OpenClaw (Agent #3), DoctorLogic AI lead scoring (Agent #4) — four AI agents, four audit trails, zero unified HIPAA policy. Gartner's August 2025 research gives organizations a 3–6 month window to define an agentic AI strategy before inheriting unmanaged agent sprawl. MuleSoft Agent Fabric is the governance layer that prevents this.

What Actually Changes

A 6-month structured proof-of-value that activates the MuleSoft platform already owned, builds the CareStack integration bridge, governs AI agents under Flex Gateway, and produces documented ROI for Norwest's board package. Not a renewal negotiation — a $25K architectural investment with milestones and a clean go/no-go at month 6.
